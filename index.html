01 / About

Yao
Yiheng

I studied Symbolic Systems at Stanford — an interdisciplinary program spanning computation, cognition, linguistics, and philosophy. Before that I worked as a software engineer at Google. The throughline has always been: how do minds (human and artificial) represent and act on the world?

My work sits at the intersection of cognitive science, machine learning, and systems engineering. On the research side I think about human causal reasoning, decision-making under uncertainty, and what it takes to build AI systems that are reliably interpretable. On the engineering side I care about performance — from GPU kernel optimization to interactive browser graphics.

I'm drawn to problems where the theoretical and the practical are in genuine tension: why does a model behave this way, what can we actually measure, and how do we build something a person would want to use? This site is a sketchpad for that kind of thinking.
